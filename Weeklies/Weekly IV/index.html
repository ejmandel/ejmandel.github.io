Cybernetics: All Watched Over by Machines of Loving Grace Pt. II
What was the cultural impact of cybernetics?
The largest cultural impact that came about in regards to cybernetics, in my opinion, was the counterculture movement in the 1970s that adopted Buckminster Fuller’s ideology and created various experimental communities. Fuller philosophized, based on cybernetic principles, that humans are only nodes in a larger universal system. He thought that we ought to be less interested in individualistic concepts and more concerned with the wellbeing of the larger system at hand. Further, Buckminster Fuller explains that, as humans, we should take into account the glorious systems utilized in nature and replicate for our own use, hence the architecture of his famous geodesic dome structure. In terms of cultural impact, those involved in the counterculture of the 1970s strongly identified with Fuller’s concepts and began to create experimental societies. They were essentially communes that saw themselves as part of a larger ecological system. Politics were essentially forbidden, and there was a general lack of hierarchy. Connectivity was valued highly.
